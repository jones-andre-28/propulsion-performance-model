## Why does increasing chamber pressure increase thrust?
7/7/26 
The force equation states F = m-dot * Ve = Pc * At * Cf which explicitly shows that the chamber pressure is proportional to the thrust (F). When the chamber pressure is higher, as it goes through the throat, it will be forced through a reduced area which will increase the speed of the air and as it exits the nozzle, which is the Ve, it creates more thrust

## What role does ambient pressure play?
7/20/26
It affects the way the thrust comes out the nozzle. 
If:
    ambient pressure = nozzle exit pressure, all of the thrust is pointed straight down meaning all of your force is moving the rocket straight up
    
    ambient pressure < nozzle exit pressure, the gas is underexpanded meaning the plume coming out the engine is in a bell like shape rather than a straight rectangular shape which decreases efficiency as some of the thrust is in various directions
    
    ambient pressure > nozzle exit pressure, it pinches the plume higher up in the nozzle resulting in a decrease in efficiency as the gas is overexpanded
